Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

How Understanding Neuroplasticity Benefits People

To put it simply, neuroplasticity is the science behind the brain being malleable and in an ever-changing state. Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity, we realise that there's truth to the old adage "practice makes permanent". The more time we spend learning or practicing something, the more we strengthen the neural pathways in our brain for that particular skill. This makes it easier to recall that information or excel at that particular task. When people understand the benefits of neuroplasticity, they are more likely to put in the effort required to improve at something because they know that it can be improved based on scientific research.

How Might We Use it to Our Benefit?

Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity, I see that putting in work every day - even just a small amount - will help strengthen those neural pathways in my brain.

Increasing Your Neuroplasticity

Reflection and journalling are great ways to improve your neuroplasticity, as you're taking time to revisit experiences and things you've learned that day. It's also more of an active thought process, as opposed to passively consuming information. Visualisation is another great tool to use for increasing neuroplasticity. Imagining yourself performing a task activates the same neurons as if you were actually performing the task.

Growth Mindset and its Relevance

Growth mindset is the belief that our skills and intelligence are not fixed. That by challenging ourselves with difficult tasks, we can grow and excel in those areas. Having a growth mindset in the context of learning, there's more incentive to work harder and embrace challenges understanding that this is where we find growth.

This was eye-opening for me, and it definitely changes how I will be approaching things as a parent moving forward. I will integrate growth mindset into my learning journey by not shying away from challenges. Doing things that are difficult and/or outside my comfort zone will help me grow as a person and should be embraced.

Applying Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset to My Learning Plan

Having the knowledge is one thing, but putting it into practice is key. Understanding how neuroplasticity works, it's clear that the more we practice a specific skill, the better our brains adapt physiologically to performing this skill. We can also benefit from imagining ourselves performing the skill, as it engages the same mechanisms in our brain. So actively reflecting on what we have learnt will help to solidify it. Developing a growth mindset through this process encourages us to perservere when things get challenging, instead of looking for an easy way out. Anyone who was ever a master at something had to go through many hours of practice, and we can get there too if we're willing to put in the hard work an embrace the struggle.
